Why SimDist

From brittle finetuning to modular adaptation

End-to-end RL finetuning in the real world often forgets useful priors from simulation. SimDist keeps global task structure fixed and updates only what changes most across domains: latent dynamics.

1. The failure mode

Sim-to-real policies fail under dynamics mismatch. Standard policy finetuning entangles representation, dynamics, and returns, forcing relearning with very little real data.

2. Modular world models

World models separate encoding, dynamics prediction, and value estimation. That structure makes transfer selective: preserve global structure, adapt only environment-specific dynamics.

3. Practical consequence

Real-world adaptation becomes supervised latent dynamics learning with online planning, avoiding long-horizon bootstrapping that destabilizes many end-to-end baselines.

Current limits and open opportunities
